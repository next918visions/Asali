Business Concept Brief · Draft 001

Asali Entertainment

Claremore, Oklahoma · Experience + Community + Culture

90s / Y2K Aesthetic Gaming + Events Hybrid Nonprofit ~600 Day Launch
01 — The Vision
An entertainment center rooted in nostalgia, community, and experience — bringing the warmth of the 90s/early 2000s to Claremore, a time people are craving to get back to. Not just a place to hang out. A place to feel something. An experience that sells a feeling: safety, joy, belonging, and the freedom of youth before the world got complicated. Built on a hybrid model — a for-profit experience layer on top of a nonprofit foundation.
02 — Brand Identity

Look & Feel

🎨

Color Palette

90s / early Y2K colors — bold teals, electric purples, warm oranges, hot pinks. Asali — inspired by the Swahili word for honey.

✨

The Feeling

Pre-recession sweetness. The early 2000s sweet spot — after the wars, before the crash. A simpler, warmer time that our generation is deeply nostalgic for.

🕹️

Pop Culture Timing

Minecraft movie, gaming revival, retro gaming culture — this moment is perfectly timed for a nostalgic, gamified entertainment space.

03 — The Space

What's Inside

Large carpet area with bean bags — chill gaming lounge vibe, Xbox/gaming consoles
Minecraft setup + sports games + dance/rhythm games (DDR-style)
Center stage / multipurpose platform — daytime use as display/activity space, evening use for events and performances
Tokens, tickets, prizes — some sold, some won, some gifted through programming
Artisan Wall — local Claremore makers contribute handmade goods (keychains, crochet, art). Each contributor's name permanently on the wall + certificate
Class / consultation space in the back — financial literacy, life skills, youth programming
Film screening capability — host film club nights, Next Vision productions, educational film study
04 — Community Flywheel

The Artisan Wall Strategy

Reach out to retired women, grandmas, local makers in Claremore — people who crochet, make keychains, do small crafts. Invite them to donate 2–3 pieces to the store. In return: their name on the wall permanently, a framed certificate, and a video recorded of them contributing. That video becomes promo content — "Look who's building this dream with us." It gets their grandkids in the door, builds word-of-mouth before opening, and creates a genuine community story. Every person who touches this store becomes an ambassador.
05 — Target Audience

Who We Serve

🧒

Junior high through high school. Claremore High School feeder. The gaming, hangout, social anchor for this age group.

🎓

Young Adults (14+)

The transition from teen to adult. Financial literacy, life skills, community. RSU students as a secondary base.

👵

Artisans, grandmas, established locals. Not buyers — contributors. Their investment brings their families.

🏘️

Claremore as a Whole

Fill the gap — there's nothing else like this here. First mover advantage in a community with real demand and no competition.

06 — Business Structure

Hybrid Model

🌱 Nonprofit Arm

Financial literacy programming
College student fund / scholarships
Youth development classes
Grant eligibility (Build in Tulsa, state grants, etc.)
Community events & outreach

💼 For-Profit Layer

Token / ticket / game revenue
Event hosting fees
Artisan merchandise sales
Next Vision sublease / collaboration
Operational cost recovery

Next Vision Connection: The nonprofit houses the community mission. Next Vision LLC operates as a for-profit collaborator — renting space, using the venue for film screenings and events, keeping books clean and separate. The nonprofit gets grants more easily; Next Vision gets a legitimate infrastructure partner. Clean books, clear lines.
07 — Funding Strategy

How We Get There

Military benefits — use every resource and benefit before separation. Healthcare, SBA loans, Boots to Business, veteran entrepreneur programs
Credit building — both partners building credit simultaneously for a stronger combined pitch
Bank relationship — existing trust at current bank. Present in person with full credentials, military history, business plan
Nonprofit grants — once 501(c)(3) established, eligible for community grants, education grants, Build in Tulsa pipeline
Selling an experience — this is not a product pitch, it's a feeling pitch. Show investors what the community walks into
